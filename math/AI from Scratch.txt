In algebra, we could have the following equation:
2x + 3 = 11

The letter 'x' is just a placeholder for a number.

Question:
What value of x makes this equation true?

2x + 3 = 11
2x = 11 - 3
2x = 8
x = 4

Multiplication with variables shows up everywhere in artificial
intelligence.

3x

The above simply means "Three times x.".

Coordinates

(0, 0) is called the origin. It is the point where the x-axis and y-axis meet.

In computer graphics systems, such as games and images, (0, 0) is instead
placed in the top-left corner of the screen.

If we have:
x = 2
y = 5

we can plot the point (2, 5) on a graph.

The first number, 2 tells us how far to move along the x-axis.

Positive values move to the right.

Negative values move to the left.

The second number, 5 tells us how far to move along the y-axis.

Positive values move up.

Negative values move down.

Coordinates are how computers represent locations.

AI often thinks of data as points in space.

Vectors

Instead of one number, we can have several:
[2, 5, 1]

This is called a vector.

A vector is just a list of numbers.

Matrices

A matrix is simply a table of numbers.

[
    [1, 2],
    [3, 4]
]

If a vector is one row of numbers, a matrix is lots of rows.
